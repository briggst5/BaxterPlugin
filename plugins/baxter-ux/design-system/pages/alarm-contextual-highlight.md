### Application examples

Alarm-driven visual treatments can be applied to content containers or specific UI elements to reinforce the impact of a condition directly where the information is displayed. By adapting elements like background and/or stroke to the alarm’s severity, the design provides immediate contextual relevance and supports faster recognition of critical states.



#### Contextual highlights on cards

When an alarm condition affects content within a card, apply a visual treatment to the card (e.g. background and stroke) that reflects the alarm’s severity to reinforces the condition’s relevance and helps users quickly recognize the severity level in context.



Examples of cards with different severity highlight visual treatment.

Example of card contextual highlight on Infusion Pump.





#### Contextual highlights on animations

When animated elements are used to communicate movement or progress that may trigger an alarm, they can inherit the same visual treatment as the alarm state (e.g., severity color) to reinforce the context of the condition. By integrating motion with consistent severity cues, the UI provides a stronger cognitive link between cause (progress/movement) and effect (alarm).



Examples of animated cues on GSS remote.
