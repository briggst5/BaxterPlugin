### Severity variants

Each severity is associated with a specific color and icon, ensuring priority is visually communicated and creating a consistent and universal user experience of interpreting alarms.





Example of High and Very low severity Alarm Banner on Spot Monitor.





### Multiple alarms

This variant is designed to be activated or turned off if multiple alarms are active or not.



When multiple alarms are active, the accordion should display the highest-severity alarm as the expanded (visible) banner.

Example of multiple alarms on Med Surg Bed.





### Style variants

These variants are designed for general use across the interface and allow for a collapsible behavior.



Example of Silenced Alarm Banner on Infusion Pump. The banner appears from the top edge, either with a “Move in” or “Instant” animation. When the user silences the alarm, it shrinks into its collapsed state which displays the remaining time until the next audible alert.
