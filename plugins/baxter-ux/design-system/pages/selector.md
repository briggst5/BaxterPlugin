## Selector



### Anatomy

The Selection Item component serves as the foundational element for constructing the more complex Selector component. It is available in different variations, as described below, which pertain to size, icon presence, and the positioning of the Selector within the user interface.

Designers are encouraged to utilize the Selection Item to create their own Selector components, provided that padding, gaps, and the general guidelines are adhered to.







### Size Variants









### Presets

The Selector component is designed to provide designers with ready-to-use, guidelines-compliant selectors composed of individual Selection Items. By utilizing these pre-defined options, designers can ensure compliance with design standards.

However, it is also possible to create custom Selectors using individual Selection Items, provided that padding, gaps, and the general guidelines are adhered to. Please refer to this documentation for guidelines and rules to ensure that the custom design remains consistent with the overall experience and visual identity.









### Selector Item



#### Interaction States

Depending on the flow or content, selections can be either mutually exclusive or allow multiple choices. For mutually exclusive selections, tapping an unselected item will change the current selection. In both scenarios, tapping on a selected item will revert it to its default state.
