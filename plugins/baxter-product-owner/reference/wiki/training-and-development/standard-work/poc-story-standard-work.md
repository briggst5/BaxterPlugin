# Overview
The purpose of the Proof-of-Concept stories: 
- retire technical risk
- demonstrate functionality
- solicit stakeholder feedback

# Proof-of-Concept Review Criteria
A Proof-of-Concept will help answer:
- Do we understand stakeholder expectations?
- Will we meet stakeholder expectations?
- Is the solution technically feasible?
  - Have technical risks been tested?
  - Does the solution align to architecture?
  - Is the solution testable?


# Writing a Proof-of-Concept Story
The following questions should be addressed in a Proof-of-Concept User Story:
- Definition
  - Clear definition of the problem, risk, or feature 
  - In-scope vs out-of-scope items 
  - Architectural constraints or standards
- Acceptance Criteria 
  - Evidence to be produced (working demo, diagrams, design documents, wireframes, etc)
  - Who needs to approve the PoC
