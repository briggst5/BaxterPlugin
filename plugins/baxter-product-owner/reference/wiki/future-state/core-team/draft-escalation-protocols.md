# Governance
This guide defines when, how, and to whom issues are escalated so we product customers, meet commitments, and maintain flow.  This guide covers product, delivery, technical, security/privacy, compliance, and resourcing issues across the Discovery -> Delivery -> Operate cycle of development.

## Principles
1. Safety & Customer First - Always seek to prevent or limit harm
1. Escalate Early, with Context - Provide evidence and options, don't wait for certainty
1. Shortest Responsible Path - Escalate to the party with the decision rights to resolve.
1. One Owner - Each escalation has a single, directly responsible individual.
1. Blameless and Transparent - Focus on facts, systems, and learning.
1. Automate Evidence - Use dashboards / pipelines / gates where possible to avoid manual collection and reviews.

## Triggers

* Customer / Value - Breach of the Boundary Agreement, 
* Flow / Delivery - Vote of no confidence at iteration midpoint, predictability < 70%
* Engineering - Unmet delivery of accountable work > 1 iteration
* Quality - Change failure rate > threshold, test coverage or pass rates drop below gates, repeated incidents
* Compliance / Regulatory - Required documentation, delivery missing for release, audit finding without owner / date
* Architecture / Tech Debt - Runway exhausted, NFRs unmet
* Resourcing / People - Role gap, sustained overload (WIP age trending upwards), jeopardizing PI objectives
