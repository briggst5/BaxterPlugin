# User Story Ownership in a Cross-Functional Platform Model

## Guiding Principle
* **Delivery** is owned by the **Agile Team** (cross-functional).
* **Backlog** is owned by the **Product Owner**.
* **Execution coordination** needs a clear focal point.

---

## Proposed Approach: Assign an “Execution Owner” per Story
A lightweight role responsible for driving the story to “done” across functions.

### Accountability
The **Execution Owner** is accountable for:
* **Acceptance Criteria:** Ensuring they are met end-to-end.
* **Cross-Functional Alignment:** Driving alignment and task breakdown.
* **Dependencies:** Identifying and resolving dependencies or blockers.
* **Visibility:** Maintaining visibility on the flow to completion.

---

## How to Identify the Execution Owner
Assign based on the **primary driver of flow**, not hierarchy:

| Scenario | Lead Role / Function |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Dominant function** | That function leads (SW, HW, RA, etc.) |
| **Integration-heavy work** | System / Lead Engineer |
| **External or regulatory dependency** | Interface owner (e.g., RA/QA) |
| **Balanced complexity** | Best-positioned [system] integrator |

---

## When is it Assigned?
1.  **Backlog Refinement:** Identify the owner.
2.  **Sprint Planning:** Confirm ownership and dependencies.
3.  **Execution (Daily/Team Sync):** Owner drives to closure.

---

## What This Is / Is Not

| ✅ What It Is | ❌ What It Is Not |
| :--- | :--- |
| Coordination focal point for flow | Not a project manager |
| Enables accountability | Not the single owner of delivery |
| Maintains team ownership | Not replacing PO or team accountability |
